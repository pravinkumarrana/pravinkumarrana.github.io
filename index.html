About me.

I’m a technology professional with over 15 years of experience spanning research, embedded algorithms, and product development. My work bridges visual computing, extended reality (XR), computer vision, eye tracking, and the transformative potential of machine learning—delivering solutions embedded in consumer products and tailored to evolving client needs.

Currently, I work as a Product Owner and Project Manager at Tobii AB, Sweden, where I lead the development of next-gen eye tracking products and solutions for XR — covering augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), and Smart Glasses. My role involves aligning development with user needs and long-term business value.

From 2016 to 2020, I served as a Technical Lead and Scrum Master in Tobii’s platform and integration algorithm team, where I also contributed as a Senior Algorithm Developer. I was involved in the development of Tobii Eye Tracking VR Development Kit and worked on integrating Tobii’s technology into, such as HTC VIVE Pro Eye, StarVR One, Qualcomm Snapdragon XR platforms, Pico Neo 3 Pro Eye, Pico Neo 2 Eye, HP Reverb G2 Omnicept Edition, and Sony PlayStation VR2.

Between 2014 and 2016, I worked on Tobii’s IS3 and IS4 eye-tracking platforms, contributing to algorithm design and optimization for the Tobii EyeChip—the first system-on-chip for eye tracking—and developing real-time camera control algorithms to support Windows Hello compliance. These platforms powered eye tracking in a variety of consumer and medical products, including PRC-Saltillo Accent devices, SteelSeries Sentry Eye Tracker, Dell Alienware laptops, and Acer Aspire V 17 Nitro.

From 2008 to 2014, I was Graduate Researcher at KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm, supervised by Prof. Markus Flierl. My research focused on 3D scene geometry estimation, view rendering algorithms, and the design of data formats for free-viewpoint television (FTV), supported in part by Ericsson AB and the ACCESS Linnaeus Centre at KTH. During this time, I also served as a Teaching Assistant at KTH for courses in image and video processing (2011–2014), multimedia signal processing (2010–2014), information theory and source coding (2009, 2012), and supervised degree projects in electrical engineering (2010).

Earlier, I taught Physics at Government Polytechnic, Koderma, India (2005–2006), before earning a Master of Technology from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Kharagpur, India, and a Master of Science in Physics from Ranchi University, India. Between the two, I taught Physics at Government Polytechnic, Koderma, India.

I’m originally from Jhumri Telaiya, a town in northern Jharkhand, India. If you enjoy Indian classical music, I invite you to discover the work of Tripti Sharma on Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube.
